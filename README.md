# Project Overview

## Problem Statement

University students and fresh graduates often struggle to find internships and entry-level jobs because existing recruitment platforms mainly target experienced professionals. Many job listings labeled as "entry-level" still require prior experience, while internship opportunities are scattered across multiple platforms with inconsistent information. Students also face difficulties verifying employers, understanding eligibility requirements, and tracking multiple applications.

Employers, on the other hand, often receive applications from unqualified candidates and have limited tools to identify suitable beginner talent efficiently.

---

## Target Users

### Primary Users

- University students
- Fresh graduates
- Beginner job seekers

### Secondary Users

- Companies
- SMEs
- Startups
- HR recruiters
- Hiring managers

---

## Proposed Solution

The **Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform** is a web application that connects students with verified employers offering internships and beginner-friendly job opportunities.

The platform enables students to:

- Register securely
- Build professional profiles
- Upload resumes and portfolios
- Search internships and entry-level jobs
- Filter beginner-friendly opportunities
- Apply online
- Track application progress
- Save interesting jobs
- Report suspicious job postings

Employers can:

- Register company accounts
- Verify their organization
- Post internship opportunities
- Review applicants
- Filter candidates by skills
- Manage recruitment through a centralized dashboard

The MVP focuses on improving trust, transparency, and accessibility for first-time job seekers.

# Lab 03 — Customer Discovery & Problem Validation

## Objectives

The objective of Lab 03 was to validate whether the selected problem identified in Lab 02 genuinely exists by collecting evidence from potential users. The team conducted customer discovery through surveys and interviews to understand users' current experiences, identify recurring pain points, and validate assumptions before designing the MVP.

---

## Customer Discovery Activities

The team prepared structured survey questions and interview sessions targeting both university students and employers.

### Target Respondents

- University students
- Fresh graduates
- SMEs
- Startup founders
- Employers involved in internship or entry-level recruitment

**Total Responses Collected:** 10

- **7 Student / Fresh Graduate Respondents**
- **3 Employer Respondents**

---

## Main Problems Identified

Customer discovery revealed several recurring problems experienced by both job seekers and employers.

### Student / Fresh Graduate Challenges

- Difficulty finding genuine internship and entry-level opportunities.
- Many "entry-level" jobs still require prior work experience.
- Unclear eligibility requirements such as language proficiency, visa status, or work permit requirements.
- Scam or fake job postings on social media platforms.
- Difficulty keeping track of multiple job applications.

### Employer Challenges

- Difficulty identifying qualified beginner candidates.
- Lack of reliable information about applicants' skills and portfolios.
- Receiving applications from unsuitable candidates.
- Time-consuming applicant screening process.

---

## Current Alternatives Used

### Students Currently Use

- LinkedIn
- JobsDB
- Facebook Groups
- Telegram
- LINE Groups
- University Career Center
- Personal referrals

### Employers Currently Use

- LinkedIn
- University Career Centers
- Facebook recruitment groups
- Internal referrals

---

## Evidence Collected

Customer responses consistently supported the team's assumptions.

The strongest evidence showed that:

- Students want a platform dedicated to internships and entry-level jobs.
- Verified employers significantly increase trust.
- Clear eligibility requirements help students decide whether to apply.
- Resume and portfolio uploads improve employer confidence.
- Application tracking helps students manage multiple applications.
- Employers prefer filtering applicants using skills and qualifications.

These findings directly influenced the MVP scope and system requirements defined in Lab 04.

---

## Assumption Validation

The assumptions identified during opportunity scanning were compared against customer responses.

### Validated Assumptions

- Students struggle to identify genuine beginner-friendly jobs.
- Employer verification increases platform trust.
- Resume and portfolio visibility influence hiring decisions.
- Application tracking is valuable for job seekers.
- Employers need filtering tools to shortlist applicants efficiently.
- Students want one platform dedicated to internships and entry-level jobs.

All major assumptions received sufficient supporting evidence from customer interviews and survey responses.

---

## Updated Problem Statement

University students and fresh graduates often struggle to find trustworthy internship and entry-level job opportunities because existing recruitment platforms mainly target experienced professionals. Job postings frequently contain unclear eligibility requirements, require previous work experience, or lack sufficient employer verification. At the same time, employers experience difficulty identifying qualified beginner candidates and efficiently screening applicants. A dedicated platform with verified employers, beginner-friendly job listings, resume and portfolio management, and application tracking can improve trust and simplify the recruitment process for both students and employers.

---

## Key Insights

The customer discovery process highlighted several important insights:

- Trust is one of the biggest factors influencing whether students apply for a job.
- Employer verification should be visible before students submit applications.
- Resume and portfolio management are essential for first-time job seekers.
- Application tracking helps students organize multiple applications.
- Employers value structured applicant profiles that include skills, education, and portfolios.
- Beginner-friendly filtering is one of the platform's strongest value propositions.

These insights became the foundation for the user personas, user stories, functional requirements, and MVP features developed in Lab 04.

# Lab 05 — Product Concept and UI/UX Wireframe

## Objectives

The objective of Lab 05 was to convert the Lab 04 system requirements and MVP plan into a clear product concept, requirement-driven screen plan, user flow diagram, use case diagram, and UI/UX wireframes.

This lab focuses on showing how the Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform will look, how users will move through the system, and how each planned screen connects to the system requirements and MVP features.

---

## Product Concept Summary

The Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform is a web-based interactive prototype designed for university students and fresh graduates who are looking for internship and entry-level job opportunities.

The platform also supports employers, startups, and SMEs that want to recruit beginner-level talent through verified company profiles, structured applicant review, and centralized application management.

The main value of the platform is to improve trust, transparency, and accessibility for first-time job seekers by providing beginner-friendly job listings, verified company information, resume/document upload, job application tracking, and suspicious job reporting.

---

## Main User Roles

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Student | Searches for internship and entry-level jobs, uploads resume/documents, applies for jobs, saves jobs, messages employers, and tracks application status |
| Employer | Creates company profile, submits company verification, posts jobs after approval, reviews applicants, messages students, and updates application status |
| Admin | Supports the prototype by reviewing company/student verification and reported job posts |

## User Flow Summary

The user flow diagram explains the main interaction paths for Student, Employer, and Admin users.

### Student Flow

Student opens the platform, selects the Student role, logs in, completes profile information, submits verification details, uploads resume/documents, browses jobs, applies filters, views job details, checks company profile, applies for a job, and tracks application status.

### Employer Flow

Employer opens the platform, selects the Employer role, logs in, creates a company profile, submits company verification, waits for approval, posts jobs after verification, views applicants, reviews applicant profiles, messages students, and updates application status.

### Admin Flow

Admin reviews student/company verification requests and checks reported job posts. The Admin can approve or reject verification and keep, hide, or remove suspicious jobs.

# Lab 06 — Business Model and Technical Architecture

## Objectives

The objective of Lab 06 was to connect the business model, user value, technical architecture, and prototype data structure of the Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform.

In this lab, the team focused on explaining how the platform creates value for students and employers, how MVP features support business and user needs, and how the prototype will be technically structured using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, sample JSON data, and browser localStorage.

---

## Lab 06 Summary

The Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform is a web-based prototype designed to help university students and fresh graduates find trustworthy internship and entry-level job opportunities.

The platform also helps employers, startups, and SMEs recruit beginner-level talent through verified company profiles, structured job postings, applicant review, and application status management.

For Lab 06, the team prepared the business model, feature-value mapping, technical architecture, system architecture diagram, data-flow diagram, data structure, and sample data plan.

## Technical Architecture

The prototype uses a simple frontend architecture.

| Layer | Technology / Component |
|---|---|
| Frontend | HTML, CSS, JavaScript |
| Data Source | Sample JSON files |
| Temporary Storage | Browser localStorage |
| Authentication | Simulated login and role selection |
| Verification | Simulated student and company verification |
| Application Process | Simulated job application submission |
| Messaging | Simulated message thread |
| Admin Review | Simulated approval, rejection, and report handling |

The prototype does not use real authentication, real database storage, or real document upload. These are treated as future enhancements.
