# Customer Discovery Questions

## Project Title
Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform

## Target Respondent
Two respondent groups will be interviewed, since the platform serves both sides of the market:
- **Group A – University students and fresh graduates** seeking internships or entry-level jobs
- **Group B – Employers, startups, or SMEs** that recruit interns or early-career talent

---

# Group A: University Students & Fresh Graduates

## Consent and Context
1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT105 course project?
2. Your participation is voluntary. We will not collect unnecessary personal data. Is it okay if we take notes?

## Recent Experience
3. Tell us about the last time you searched for an internship or entry-level job.
4. What were you trying to do at that time — find a specific role, explore options, or something else?
5. What happened from start to finish, from where you looked to whether you applied or got a response?

## Current Workaround
6. How do you usually search for internships or entry-level jobs now?
7. What tools, apps, websites, career centers, or personal networks do you currently use?
8. What is difficult, slow, confusing, risky, or frustrating about the current method (e.g. too few internships listed, unclear if a role suits beginners, hard to tell if a company is legitimate)?

## Pain Severity and Consequence
9. How often do you run into this problem when job or internship hunting?
10. How serious is this problem on a scale of 1–5? Why?
11. What happens if this problem is not solved — for example, do you miss opportunities, delay graduation plans, or settle for less suitable roles?

## Feature Expectations
12. What features would matter most to you in a platform built specifically for student internships and entry-level jobs (e.g. resume/portfolio upload, verified company badges, application tracking, direct messaging with employers)?
13. What would make you trust that a job or company posted on such a platform is genuine?

## Closing
14. Is there anything else we should understand about this problem?
15. May we use your anonymized response as part of our course project evidence?

---

# Group B: Employers, Startups & SMEs

## Consent and Context
1. May we ask you a few questions for our ICT105 course project?
2. Your participation is voluntary. We will not collect unnecessary personal data. Is it okay if we take notes?

## Recent Experience
3. Tell us about the last time your organization tried to recruit an intern or entry-level employee.
4. What were you trying to achieve — fill a specific role, build a talent pipeline, or something else?
5. What happened from start to finish, from posting the opportunity to hiring (or not hiring) someone?

## Current Workaround
6. How does your organization usually find and recruit student or early-career talent now?
7. What tools, platforms, university partnerships, or networks do you currently use?
8. What is difficult, slow, confusing, or frustrating about reaching qualified student candidates through your current methods?

## Pain Severity and Consequence
9. How often does your organization need to recruit interns or entry-level hires?
10. How serious is this recruiting challenge on a scale of 1–5? Why?
11. What happens if this problem is not solved — for example, do positions stay unfilled longer, do you get unqualified applicants, or does it cost extra time or money?

## Feature Expectations
12. What features would matter most to your organization in a platform built specifically for early-career recruitment (e.g. applicant filtering, verified student profiles, direct messaging, company verification badges)?
13. What would make your organization trust and want to post opportunities on a new platform like this?

## Closing
14. Is there anything else we should understand about this problem?
15. May we use your anonymized response as part of our course project evidence?
