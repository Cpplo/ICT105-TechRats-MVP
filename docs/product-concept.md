# Product Concept

## 1. Project Title
Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform

## 2. Target User
Primary user: university students and fresh graduates seeking internship or entry-level job opportunities that match their beginner skill level.
Secondary user: companies, startups, or SMEs recruiting early-career talent.
Supporting role (prototype only): Admin, who demonstrates verification and reported-job review.

## 3. Problem Statement
University students and fresh graduates often struggle to find internship and entry-level job opportunities that match their skills and qualifications, because most existing recruitment platforms (LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed, Glassdoor, JobThai, career pages, social media groups) primarily target experienced professionals, contain limited internship listings, or make it difficult to tell whether a role is truly suitable for a beginner. At the same time, employers find it challenging to reach and screen qualified student talent through a platform specifically designed for early-career recruitment, and often rely on manual, unstructured methods such as email and Excel trackers.

## 4. Proposed IT Product
A web-based recruitment platform, built as an interactive prototype, where university students and fresh graduates can register with a university email, create profiles, upload resumes and supporting documents, browse and filter verified internship and entry-level job opportunities, message employers, apply directly, and track application status. Employers can register a company, complete company verification, and — only once verified — post job vacancies, review applicant profiles and documents, message students, and update application status. An Admin Verification Page demonstrates how student/company verification and reported job posts would be reviewed.

## 5. Core Value Proposition
For students: a platform built specifically for beginners, where every listing can be filtered for true entry-level suitability, every company is verified before applying, and every application is tracked in one place — replacing scattered searches across LinkedIn, Facebook groups, and personal notes.
For employers: a focused channel to reach early-career talent through verified company status and structured applicant profiles — replacing manual screening through unstructured messages and spreadsheets.

## 6. MVP Scope
The MVP will focus on:
- Simulated registration, login, and role selection for Student or Employer (FR-01, FR-02)
- Student profile management, verification detection, and resume/document upload (FR-03, FR-05, FR-06)
- Employer registration and company verification detection, with job posting blocked until verified (FR-07, FR-08, FR-30)
- Verified badge display for students and companies (FR-29)
- Internship/entry-level job posting for verified employers (FR-10)
- Browse, search, and filter jobs by beginner-friendly status and eligibility (FR-11, FR-13)
- Job detail viewing and company profile viewing (FR-14, FR-15)
- Job application submission using resume and documents (FR-16)
- Application Tracker and saved jobs (FR-17, FR-18)
- Employer applicant management, applicant filtering, and status updates (FR-09, FR-19, FR-20)
- Direct messaging between students and employers (FR-21)
- Job reporting and Admin Verification Page for report handling (FR-22, FR-23)
- Student and Employer dashboard/summary views (FR-12)
- Data storage and record management using local/sample data (FR-04)
- Confirmation/error messages, input validation, responsive and privacy-aware interface (FR-24, FR-25, FR-26, FR-27)

## 7. Out of Scope for Current Semester
- Real production authentication or real database
- Official university/company verification through external institutions or APIs
- AI-assisted student/company verification
- AI-powered job recommendations or AI resume feedback
- Interview scheduling or video interviews
- Email notifications and calendar integration
- Mobile application and push notifications
- University Single Sign-On (SSO)
- Employer analytics dashboard
- Career roadmap generation
