# Problem Notes

## Project Title
Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform

## Target Respondents

| Type | Description | Why Selected |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | University students searching for internships | They directly experience problems finding beginner-friendly internships before graduation. |
| Primary | Fresh graduates searching for entry-level jobs | They face difficulty identifying true entry-level roles that do not require prior full-time experience. |
| Primary | International students searching for internships in Bangkok/Thailand | They need clear information about eligibility, language requirements, and work conditions before applying. |
| Secondary | Employers, startups, SMEs, and HR staff recruiting interns or entry-level employees | They represent the employer side and experience problems screening, comparing, and tracking student applicants. |

## Target Respondent Profile

### Group A: Students and Fresh Graduates
- Age range: 19–23 years old
- University students or recent graduates
- Searching for internships, part-time internships, or entry-level jobs
- Interested in fields such as ICT, software development, AI/ML, data analysis, marketing, admin, or IT support
- Currently using general platforms such as LinkedIn, JobsDB, Facebook groups, LINE groups, company career pages, university career centers, and personal networks

### Group B: Employers, Startups, SMEs, and HR Staff
- Startup founders, SME owners, or HR staff
- Recruiting interns or early-career employees
- Hiring for roles such as UI/UX intern, marketing intern, admin intern, IT support intern, or junior entry-level roles
- Currently using Facebook groups, LINE groups, university partnerships, email, Excel trackers, referrals, and LinkedIn

## Consequences of Unsolved Problem

- Students may apply late, miss suitable internships, or accept unrelated work because beginner-friendly roles are hard to identify (R001).
- Final-year students may delay their internship plan and graduate with less practical experience (R002).
- Students may waste time or share personal information with untrusted recruiters because job posts are vague or suspicious (R003).
- Fresh graduates may settle for unrelated jobs and lose confidence when “entry-level” roles still require experience (R004).
- International students may avoid applying even when interested because eligibility and language requirements are unclear (R005).
- Students may forget deadlines, contacts, or company replies when applications are tracked manually (R006).
- Beginner students may wait too long before applying or study the wrong topics because requirements are difficult to understand (R007).
- Employers may spend more time screening applicants and comparing portfolios manually (R008, R009).
- HR teams may miss good candidates because candidate information is inconsistent and tracking is slow (R010).

## Key Problem Statement

University students and fresh graduates struggle to find trustworthy, beginner-friendly internships and entry-level jobs because existing job platforms mix student roles with experienced roles, provide unclear requirements, and offer limited verification or application tracking. At the same time, employers, startups, and SMEs spend too much time manually screening student applicants because candidate information, portfolios, skills, and availability are not presented in a structured way.

---

## Revised Product Opportunity

A student-focused internship and entry-level job platform can solve this problem by connecting verified students and verified employers in one structured system. The platform should prioritize beginner-role filtering, verified company profiles, student portfolios, skill tags, application tracking, eligibility clarity, and employer screening tools.

---

## MVP Feature Priorities

| Priority | MVP Feature | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| High | Internship-only and true entry-level job filters | Directly solves the most common student problem: unclear beginner suitability. |
| High | Verified company profiles and trust badges | Reduces scam risk and increases student confidence. |
| High | Student profile with CV, portfolio, skills, and project links | Helps employers compare students more easily. |
| High | Application tracker with status and deadline reminders | Helps students and employers avoid missed deadlines and manual tracking. |
| Medium | Skill match checklist | Helps students understand whether they are ready to apply. |
| Medium | Eligibility, language, work mode, and location fields | Helps international and beginner students decide whether to apply. |
| Medium | Employer dashboard and applicant filtering | Reduces employer screening time. |
| Low | Career roadmap, CV templates, and example projects | Useful support feature, but can be added after the core job-matching system. |

---

## Validation Decision

The updated interview evidence supports continuing with the **Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform** idea. The strongest validated problems are:

1. Students cannot easily find beginner-friendly internships and true entry-level jobs.
2. Students need more trust and verification before applying.
3. Students and employers both need better application tracking.
4. Employers need structured profiles and filtering to compare student candidates.
5. International students need clearer eligibility and language information.

The project should not focus only on listing more jobs. The main value should be **trusted, beginner-friendly, structured, and trackable internship/entry-level recruitment** for both students and employers.
