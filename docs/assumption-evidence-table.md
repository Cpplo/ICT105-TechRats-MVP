# Assumption vs Evidence Table

## Project Title

Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform

## Purpose

This table separates what the team believes (assumptions) from what users actually said during customer discovery. The evidence is based on **10 interview responses (R001–R010)** collected in Lab 03.

---

## Assumption vs Evidence

| # | Team Assumption | Evidence Found | Source | Validated? |
| - | --------------- | -------------- | ------ | ---------- |
| 1 | Students struggle because general job platforms are not designed for beginner internships. | R001, R002, R004, and R007 mentioned unclear requirements, senior-level roles, or confusion about whether positions were suitable for beginners. | R001, R002, R004, R007 | Validated |
| 2 | Trust and verification are major reasons students hesitate to apply. | R003, R005, and R008 mentioned scam risks, unclear eligibility, and the need for verified employer or student profiles before applying. | R003, R005, R008 | Validated |
| 3 | Application tracking is a real pain for both students and employers. | R006 missed application deadlines and tracked applications manually, while R010 managed applicants using email and Excel spreadsheets. | R006, R010 | Validated |
| 4 | Employers mainly need more applicants, not better screening. | R008, R009, and R010 received applicants but struggled with candidate quality, portfolio comparison, and inconsistent applicant information. This indicates employers need better screening rather than simply more applicants. | R008, R009, R010 | **Revised** |
| 5 | International students need clearer eligibility and language information. | R005 avoided applying for internships because visa eligibility and language requirements were unclear. | R005 | Validated |

## Key Insights

- Existing recruitment platforms make it difficult for students to identify true internship and beginner-friendly opportunities.
- Trust is a major factor influencing job applications, highlighting the need for verified company profiles and transparent employer information.
- Both students and employers experience challenges tracking applications, indicating the need for application tracking and employer dashboards.
- Employers value structured student profiles, portfolios, and filtering tools more than simply receiving a larger number of applicants.
- International students require clearer information about eligibility, language requirements, and work conditions before applying.
