# Lab 02: IT Opportunity Scanning

## What We Completed

1. Reviewed the three venture ideas from Lab 01 (AI Knowledge Management System, Digital Identity & Credential Verification Platform, and Student Freelancer Marketplace Platform) and evaluated their problem areas, target users, existing alternatives, and possible technology solutions.
2. Conducted opportunity scanning by identifying six potential IT opportunities that address real-world problems experienced by students, universities, and employers, then analyzed each idea based on customer needs and technical feasibility.
3. Completed the NUF (New, Useful, Feasible) Scoring Matrix to compare all opportunities objectively and selected the highest-ranked project for semester development.

## Selected Opportunity

**Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform**

## Why We Selected It

Using the NUF scoring method, the **Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform** achieved the highest overall score because it addresses a real and recurring problem experienced by university students and fresh graduates. Many respondents struggle to find genuine internship and entry-level opportunities since existing recruitment platforms mainly target experienced professionals.

The idea also received a high feasibility score because the MVP can be developed as a web application using modern frontend and backend technologies while focusing on core recruitment workflows such as employer verification, job posting, application tracking, and applicant management. The project aligns well with the team's technical capabilities and can be completed within the semester.

## What We Rejected

**AI Knowledge Management System** — Although the idea demonstrated strong innovation by incorporating AI-powered document retrieval, it requires complex AI integration, vector databases, and advanced backend infrastructure that exceed the scope of this semester's MVP.

**Digital Identity & Credential Verification Platform** — While useful for educational institutions and employers, this project depends heavily on secure credential verification, encryption, digital signatures, and institutional partnerships. These requirements significantly increase implementation complexity and make it less feasible for a semester prototype.

## What We Learned

1. Selecting a project should balance innovation, usefulness, and feasibility rather than focusing on novelty alone.
2. Customer problems should drive technology decisions instead of choosing technology first.
3. The NUF scoring framework provided an objective way to compare different opportunities and justify the final project selection.
4. A focused MVP that solves one validated problem is more achievable and valuable than attempting to build a large system with too many features.

# Weekly Venture Logbook

## Lab 03: Customer Problem Discovery

### What We Completed

1. Designed customer discovery survey questions and interview templates for university students, fresh graduates, and employers.
2. Collected **10 customer discovery responses**, consisting of **7 university students or fresh graduates** and **3 employer representatives** from SMEs and companies.
3. Analyzed customer feedback to identify recurring pain points, validated assumptions through the Assumption vs Evidence Table, and prepared the Customer Discovery Summary to determine whether the project direction should continue.

### What We Learned About the Problem

1. University students and fresh graduates find it difficult to identify genuine internship and entry-level opportunities because many job postings still require previous experience despite being labeled as "entry-level."
2. Existing recruitment platforms such as LinkedIn, JobsDB, Facebook Groups, Telegram, and university career channels provide opportunities, but they do not effectively support beginner job seekers or clearly indicate whether positions are suitable for students.
3. Employers also experience difficulties reaching qualified beginner candidates and want an easier way to identify applicants through structured student profiles, resumes, portfolios, and verified information.

### What Evidence We Collected

- **Number of respondents/interviews:** 10 respondents (7 university students/fresh graduates and 3 employer representatives)
- **Evidence file:** `data/Customer_Discovery_Responses_Updated.xlsx`
- **Key repeated pattern:** Students struggle to identify trustworthy beginner-friendly opportunities, while employers need an efficient way to discover suitable entry-level candidates with verified profiles and relevant skills.

### What We Changed Based on Evidence

Based on the customer discovery findings, the team refined the project into the **Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform**. The validated evidence shifted our focus from general recruitment toward helping students and fresh graduates discover verified internship and entry-level opportunities more efficiently.

The team also identified several core MVP features, including company verification, beginner-friendly job filtering, student profile and portfolio management, application tracking, employer applicant management, and clear eligibility information for each job posting.

### Problems or Difficulties

1. Although student participation was high, collecting responses from employers required additional outreach because of their limited availability.
2. Some respondents had different levels of internship or recruitment experience, requiring follow-up questions to better understand their specific challenges.
3. Additional feedback may still be required in future validation to better understand long-term employer recruitment needs and platform adoption.
