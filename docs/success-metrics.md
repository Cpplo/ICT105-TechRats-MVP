# Success Metrics and Decision Rules

## Project Title

Student Internship & Entry-Level Job Platform

---

## 1. Success Metrics

| Metric ID | Metric | Target | How to Measure | Related Requirement |
| ---------- | ------------------------------ | ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| M-01 | Homepage understanding | 90% or higher | Participants correctly explain the platform's purpose | FR-01, FR-02 |
| M-02 | Registration completion rate | 90% or higher | Successful registrations / total participants | FR-01, FR-02 |
| M-03 | Profile completion rate | 85% or higher | Completed profiles / total participants | FR-03, FR-04 |
| M-04 | Verification submission rate | 90% or higher | Successful verification submissions / total participants | FR-05, FR-06 |
| M-05 | Job search success rate | 90% or higher | Participants who successfully find a suitable job | FR-11, FR-12 |
| M-06 | Company trust recognition | 85% or higher | Participants correctly identify verified companies | FR-13, FR-14 |
| M-07 | Job application success rate | 80% or higher | Successful job applications / total participants | FR-15, FR-16 |
| M-08 | Dashboard usability rating | Average 4 out of 5 | Feedback form rating | FR-17 |
| M-09 | Overall usability rating | Average 4 out of 5 | Overall satisfaction score from feedback form | FR-01 – FR-17 |
| M-10 | Feature usefulness rating | Average 4 out of 5 | Feedback form rating | MVP Features |

---

## 2. Decision Rules

| Result | Decision |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| All metrics meet target | Continue to the final implementation and refinement of the prototype. |
| One or two metrics fail | Improve the affected user flow, interface design, navigation, or feature implementation, then conduct another usability test. |
| Three or more metrics fail | Revisit the functional requirements, user stories, MVP feature priorities, and prototype design before continuing development. |

---

## 3. Expected Outcome

The MVP experiment is considered successful if participants can complete the primary student workflow—including registration, profile completion, verification submission, job browsing, company verification checking, job application, and dashboard navigation—with minimal assistance while providing positive usability and feature usefulness ratings.

The collected feedback will be used to improve the final prototype before implementation and final project submission.
